How it works · Is this private?
Encrypted, end to end. Every message, file and call is scrambled in transit by WebRTC. Only the two of you hold the keys.
Direct. Data travels browser-to-browser. It doesn’t pass through our servers.
Relay only if needed. If a network blocks the direct link, an encrypted relay passes it along — it still can’t read a thing.
Nothing saved. No accounts, no history. Close the tab and it’s gone.
You approve. Nobody connects until you tap Accept.
A small directory just helps two browsers find each other. It never sees your chats. We use privacy-friendly, cookieless analytics that count visits and country only — never your messages, files or calls.
What can I do here?
Text chat. Send encrypted messages in real time.
Voice call. Talk with clear audio — no phone number required.
Video call. See each other face to face.
Share your screen. Present during a call — you can even switch it on mid-call.
Send files. Photos, documents, anything — delivered directly, never uploaded to a server.
Once connected: tap the green camera for video, the purple phone for voice, and the paperclip to send a file.
Who made this and why?

I started this just to call my wife and family, without some app sitting in the middle, listening in. It worked so well that I figured everyone should have it.

So I made the code public: free to read, free to run, free to build on. Talking to the people you love shouldn’t cost you your privacy.
